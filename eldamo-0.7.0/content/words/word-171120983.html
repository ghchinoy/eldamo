AT. labialized velars became labials; [kw|gw|ŋgw|ŋkw|ŋw-] > [p|b|mb|mp|m-]

The early change of labialized velars to labials is extremely well documented (PE18/104; VT41/8; WJ/374-375), and it is characteristic of the Telerin branch of the Eldarin languages. As a result, Telerin and Sindarin had initial p-, m- where Quenya had qu- [kw], nw- [ŋw]. Similar though less comprehensive changes occurred in the Celtic branch of Indo-European languages (WG/§89i, §92ii, §92v). The change of [ŋw] > simple [m] occurred only initially, because already in Primitive Elvish medial [ŋw] became [ŋgw], so that the result in Ancient Telerin was [mb]. Tolkien also indicated this change would occur for aspirated [kʰw] > AT. [pʰ] > S., T. [f] (PE19/18), but there are no attested examples.

Conceptual Development: This phonetic rule dates back to Solosimpi, the earliest form of Telerin (PE12/16-17; QL/38), but in the early Gnomish of the 1910s, the only similar change was that non-initial [kʷ] become [p] (HPG/§2.7). At this early stage, initial [kʷ] was preserved in Gnomish.

Tolkien introduced this sound change into the Early Noldorin of the 1920s, as G. cweth “word” (GL/28) >> ᴱN. peth (PE13/152). In the 1920s through 1940s, there was no direct relationship between the Telerin and Noldorin languages, so this phonetic rule must have been a parallel change, though perhaps Tolkien imagined the two languages influencing each other at their earliest stages.

In the 1950s when Tolkien reconceived Noldorin as Sindarin, he also made Sindarin a member of the Telerin branch, which allowed him to postulate this development as occurring in the ancient common ancestor of the two languages. Simplified formulations like this helped motivate Tolkien to change Noldorin into Sindarin.
