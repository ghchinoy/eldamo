S. Amon Rûdh, loc. “Bald Hill”

The hill where Mîm lived, translated “Bald Hill” (S/204). It is a combination of amon “hill” and rûdh “bald”.

Conceptual Development: In Silmarillion revisions from the 1950s-60s, this hill as first named Amon Carab “Hill of the Hat” along with several other similar forms (WJ/187).

      S. Amon Carab, loc. “Hill of the Hat”

      An earlier name of Amon Rûdh that appeared in various forms, one of which (Amon Carab) was translated “Hill of the Hat” (WJ/187). It seems to be a combination of amon “hill” and carab “hat”, not second of which is not otherwise attested.
