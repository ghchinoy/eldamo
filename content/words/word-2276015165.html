Q. **[w] became [v] after [l], [r]; [{lr}w] > [{lr}v]

In a discussion of Noldorin versus Vanyarin phonetic developments (PE17/129), Tolkien said: “in Q. both rv (< rb) and rw existed: both in EQ [Exhilic Quenya] > rv. In Q. lb and lw existed, and then both became lv”. This discussion seems to have been inspired by Tolkien’s decision to change the suffix -lmo >> -lvo (genitive of -lvë) in the phrase elen síla lúmenn’ omentielvo between the 1st and 2nd editions of The Lord of the Rings. Later in the same notes (PE17/130), Tolkien states that -lvë developed from the primitive pronoun ✶we “we (inclusive)”, which became the independent pronoun ve². Tolkien needed to explain how lw > lv, and this new phonetic rule provided this explanation.

Unfortunately, aside from the case of -lwe > -lve, there are no clear examples of this phonetic rule elsewhere in Tolkien’s writing, and in fact there remain plenty of examples of unmodified rw and lw. Where rv and lv do appear, they can be easily derived from primitive ✶rb, lb instead. Elsewhere he indicated rw, lw were preserved (PE19/81, item ix). It seems likely the general change of ✶rw, lw > rv, lv was a transient idea, later abandoned by Tolkien.

This means we still need to justifiy -lve, but this can be explained by analogy with the independent pronoun ve. These ideas came out of a discussion with Shihali on the Vinyë Lambengolmor Discord server on 2018-01-28.
