S. Lammoth, loc. “Great Echo”

The valley where Morgoth bellowed in pain under the attack of Ungoliant, translated “Great Echo” (S/106), so named because echoes of his cries could still be heard (S/80). It is a combination of lamm “echo” with the augmentative suffix -oth. As pointed out by Christoper Tolkien, this name is undoubtedly related to NS. lóm “echo” (SA/lóm).
