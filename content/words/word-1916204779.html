Ad. manô n. (weak-II gendered common) “spirit”

A noun translated “spirit” and fully declined as an example of a Weak II noun (SD/438). It appeared with both a short a (SD/424) and long â (SD/438). Given its ending -ô, it might be a masculine-noun, but it seems unlikely that spirits would only be male. This lexicon assumes it is a common-noun instead. It is probably related to Q. manu “departed spirit” as suggested by various authors (AAD/19, AL/Adûnaic, EotAL/MAN).
