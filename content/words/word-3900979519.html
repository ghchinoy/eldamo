Ad. uruk n. (strong-I gendered common) “orc, goblin”

A noun translated “goblin, orc” and fully declined as an example of a Strong I noun (SD/436). Carl Hostetter and Patrick Wynne suggested (AAD/24) this form of the word may be a derivative of the contemporaneous Elvish root ᴹ√OROK. In later writings it appeared as Ad. urku/urkhu (WJ/390), a derivative of the Elvish root √RUK¹ “terrible shapes”. This lexicon retains the form uruk because of its many attested inflections.
