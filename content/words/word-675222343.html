Ilk. breth n. “(beech) mast”

Ilk. breth n. “(beech) mast”

A word for “mast” developed from the primitive form ᴹ✶b’rethā or *✶berethā (Ety/BERÉTH), because in Ilkorin an unstressed vowel vanished if it produced favored initial consonant combinations. The primitive word originally was originally associated with beech trees, and the Ilkorin meaning was likely due to the use of these trees for masts in ship-building practices of Beleriand. The normal Ilkorin word for “beech” is galbreth or neldor.
