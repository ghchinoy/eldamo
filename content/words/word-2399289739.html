S. final [i] intruded into preceding syllable; [-V̆Ci|-Sĭ] > [-ViC|-Sø]
@@@ i-intrusion (or y-intrusion), part of a set of i-affection phonetic rules suggested to me in a private chat by Elaran on 2018-08-25. I’ve adopted these rules here without modification. A note on PE17/148 indicate Tolkien also considered having w-intrusion, but there is no evidence of it outside this note.

      Order (02400)

      After 01200 short [i], [u] became [e], [o] preceding final [a] ᴹ✶gilya > N. geil
      ᴹ✶ulyā > œil > N. eil             		Ety/GIL
      Ety/ULU
      After 01600 [ai] became [oe] or [ae] ᴹ✶galyā > N. gail
      ᴹ✶(a)ranı̯ā > rhain > N. rhein             		Ety/KAL
      EtyAC/RAN
      Before 02700 [ei] (sometimes) became [ai] in final syllables ᴹ✶bányā > N. bein
      ᴹ✶kalaryā > N. celeir             		Ety/BAN
      Ety/KAL
      Before 05600 [œ] became [e] ᴹ✶tal-runya > tellœin > N. tellen Ety/RUN
