Ilk. brith n. “broken stones, gravel”

A word for “broken stones, gravel” developed from the primitive form ᴹ✶b’rittē or *✶birittē (Ety/BIRÍT), because in Ilkorin an unstressed vowel vanished if it produced favored initial consonant combinations.
